# Assignmate — Complete Guide

Assignmate converts typed text into realistic handwritten PDFs that match your own handwriting style. This guide walks you through every step required to set up your account, generate your personal handwriting font using Calligraphr, and produce your first handwritten assignment.

Read this guide fully before starting. The Calligraphr section in particular requires attention to detail — skipping steps or rushing through it will result in a poor quality font that does not resemble your actual handwriting.

## 1. How to Use Assignmate

### 1.1 Create Your Account

Go to the Assignmate homepage and click Get Started. You will be taken to the registration page. Enter your full name, a valid email address, and a password that is at least eight characters long.

After submitting the registration form, check your email inbox for a verification message from Assignmate. Open that email and click the verification link. This activates your account. If you do not see the email within two minutes, check your spam or junk folder.

Once your account is verified, return to the Assignmate homepage and log in with your email and password. You will be taken to your dashboard.

Your dashboard is the central hub of the application. From here you can see the status of your handwriting profile, view your recent assignments, start a new assignment, and manage your account settings. Everything in Assignmate is private and tied to your individual account. No one else can see your assignments or handwriting profile.

---

### 1.2 Upload Your Handwriting Photo

Before Assignmate can render assignments in your handwriting, it needs a photo of how you write. This photo is analyzed by the AI to extract your personal style characteristics, including your letter slant, spacing between letters and words, stroke weight, and the natural irregularities in your writing.

**How to prepare your handwriting sample**

Write at least four to six full sentences on plain, unlined white paper. Do not use lined paper for this step because the lines interfere with the AI analysis. Use the same pen you use in everyday writing. A ballpoint pen or a gel pen with dark ink works best.

Write in your completely natural style. Do not try to write neater than usual or change your style in any way. The goal is to capture how you actually write every day, not how you write when you are trying to impress someone. The more natural and authentic your sample is, the more accurate your generated assignments will look.

**How to photograph your handwriting sample**

Place the paper on a flat surface with no creases or folds. Use natural daylight or a bright indoor lamp. Make sure there are no shadows crossing the page.

Hold your phone or camera directly above the paper so the camera lens is parallel to the page. Do not photograph from an angle. Every word and letter must be clearly in focus. Take the photo in the highest resolution your device allows.

Before uploading, look at the photo on your screen. All letters must be clearly legible, the page must be flat with no distortion, and the image must be sharp with no motion blur.

**Uploading the photo**

From your dashboard, click the Upload Handwriting Photo button. A file selector will open. Choose your photo. Accepted file formats are JPG, PNG, and WEBP. The maximum file size is 10 MB.

After you select the file, Assignmate will upload it and begin AI analysis. This process takes approximately 10 to 20 seconds. Do not close the browser tab while analysis is running. When analysis is complete, you will see a confirmation message on your dashboard confirming your handwriting profile is ready.

You can only have one active handwriting profile at a time. If you upload a new photo, it will replace your existing profile. Your previously generated assignments are not affected by this change.

---

### 1.3 Generate an Assignment

From your dashboard, click New Assignment. A new page will open with a text input area and a panel of settings on the right side.

**Writing your prompt**

In the text input area, type your question, essay topic, or assignment description. Be as specific as possible. Include the subject, the academic level, the approximate word count you need, and any specific points the assignment should cover.

Examples of effective prompts:

- Write a 400-word essay on the causes of the First World War for a Year 10 history class. Include three main causes and a conclusion.
- Explain the process of photosynthesis in simple language suitable for a Grade 7 science assignment. Use three paragraphs.
- Answer the following question in 200 words: What were the main achievements of the Renaissance period?
- Write a short story of approximately 300 words about a young explorer who discovers a hidden cave. This is for an English creative writing class.

The more detail you include in your prompt, the better the generated content will be. Vague prompts produce generic results.

**Adjusting settings before generating**

Before clicking generate, review the settings panel. You can adjust the following options:

Paper Style — Choose from lined notebook paper, plain white paper, or college-ruled paper. Lined notebook paper is the most commonly used for school assignments. College-ruled paper has narrower lines and fits more text per page.

Ink Color — Choose from black, blue, or dark blue. Black is standard for most written assignments. Blue is commonly used for handwritten work in many countries.

Font Size — Choose from small, medium, or large. Medium closely resembles average adult handwriting size. Use small if you tend to write compactly, or large if your natural handwriting is on the bigger side.

Line Spacing — Choose from tight, normal, or wide. This controls how much vertical space appears between each line of handwriting on the rendered page.

Page Margins — Choose from narrow, standard, or wide. Standard margins are appropriate for most school assignments.

Once you are satisfied with the settings, click the Generate button. The AI will write a full, well-structured response to your prompt and render it using your handwriting font on the paper style you selected. Generation typically takes between 15 and 45 seconds depending on the length of the assignment.

---

### 1.4 Preview and Download the PDF

After generation is complete, you will be taken to the preview page. This shows the complete rendered assignment exactly as it will appear in the downloaded PDF. You can scroll through all pages of the document.

If anything looks wrong or you want to adjust the appearance, use the settings panel on the right side of the preview page to change options like ink color, paper style, or font size. Click Re-render to apply your changes. The preview will update within a few seconds.

When you are satisfied with the result, click Download PDF. The file will download to your device. The PDF is also automatically saved to your account and can be re-downloaded at any time from your Assignment History page.

---

### 1.5 Assignment History

Every assignment you generate is saved to your account automatically. To view your history, click Assignment History in the navigation menu on your dashboard.

Each entry in your history shows the original prompt you entered, the date the assignment was generated, the paper style used, and a download button to retrieve the PDF again.

Assignments and their PDF files are automatically deleted after 30 days to save storage space. If you want to keep a specific assignment permanently, open it from your history page and click the Save button. Saved assignments are never automatically deleted.

## 2. How to Generate Your Calligraphr Font

Calligraphr is a free online service that converts your handwriting into a real font file in TTF format. This font file is what Assignmate uses to render text in your personal handwriting style when generating assignments.

This section must be completed before Assignmate can produce assignments in your handwriting. Without a font file, the application cannot replicate your personal style.

The Calligraphr process has seven steps. Each step must be completed in order. Do not skip any step.

---

### 2.1 Create a Calligraphr Account

Open a web browser and go to the following address:

```
https://www.calligraphr.com
```

On the Calligraphr homepage, click Start for free. You will be taken to the registration page. Enter your email address and choose a password, or sign up using a Google account if that option is available.

After registering, check your email for a verification message and click the link inside to activate your account. Log in to Calligraphr. You will be taken to the Calligraphr dashboard.

The free plan on Calligraphr allows you to create a font with up to 75 characters. This is more than enough for Assignmate, which requires only the 26 lowercase letters, 26 uppercase letters, the digits 0 through 9, and basic punctuation. You do not need to purchase a paid plan.

**Helpful video resources**

Search YouTube for the following terms to find video tutorials that walk through the Calligraphr process visually:

- "Calligraphr tutorial handwriting font"
- "How to create your own handwriting font Calligraphr"
- "Calligraphr complete guide 2024"

These searches will return multiple clear video walkthroughs from independent creators that demonstrate the full process from start to finish.

---

### 2.2 Download the Character Template

The character template is a printable sheet containing empty boxes, one for each letter or character you will handwrite. Calligraphr uses the filled-in boxes to identify and extract your individual letter shapes.

From your Calligraphr dashboard, click Templates in the top navigation bar. This opens the template generator page.

**Template settings to configure**

Character selection — Select the following character groups to include in your template:

- A to Z (uppercase letters)
- a to z (lowercase letters)
- 0 to 9 (numbers)
- Basic punctuation, which includes the period, comma, exclamation mark, question mark, apostrophe, hyphen, and quotation marks

Background — Select Helper Lines. This adds light guidelines inside each character box that help you keep your letters consistently sized and positioned. Do not select Blank as it makes it much harder to write consistently.

Format — Select PNG. PNG files are easier to work with on most devices and produce clean results when uploaded back to Calligraphr. You can also choose PDF if you plan to print directly from the file.

Paper size — Select A4 if you are located outside the United States, or Letter if you are in the United States or Canada.

After configuring these settings, click Create Template. Then click Download Template and save the file to your computer in a location you will remember, such as your Downloads folder or Desktop.

The template may consist of multiple pages depending on how many characters you selected.

---

### 2.3 Print and Fill In the Template

This is the most critical step in the entire Calligraphr process. The quality of your completed template directly determines the quality of your final font. Take this step seriously and do not rush it.

**Printing the template**

Print each page of the template on plain white paper. Do not print on coloured paper, textured paper, or tracing paper. Use standard 80gsm or 90gsm white printer paper.

If you do not have access to a printer, see the alternative method at the end of this section.

**Writing tools**

Use a black or dark blue ballpoint pen or a fine-tip pen such as a 0.5mm or 0.7mm fineliner. Do not use pencil under any circumstances. Pencil marks are too light and create poor quality font data. Do not use a thick marker or highlighter.

**Rules for filling in the template**

Read all of these rules before you begin writing.

Write each character naturally inside its box. The character must be completely contained within the box boundaries. Do not let any part of the letter touch or cross the outer border of the box, as this causes extraction errors.

Write in your completely natural handwriting style. Do not try to write more neatly or differently from how you normally write. The font will look strange if you write differently from your normal style.

Keep your letter sizes consistent across all pages. The height and width of similar letters should be approximately the same from box to box.

If you make a mistake in a box, do not attempt to correct it with correction fluid or by crossing it out. Print a new copy of that page and redo it.

Fill in every single box. Missing characters will appear as empty spaces in your generated assignments.

Do not write the same letter twice with different styles. Every letter must be written in a single consistent style.

Take breaks if your hand gets tired. Fatigue causes your handwriting to change and become inconsistent, which produces a lower quality font.

**Alternative method if you do not have a printer**

If you cannot print the template, you can fill it in digitally. Open the downloaded PNG file on a tablet or iPad that has a stylus or digital pen. Use a drawing application such as Procreate, Adobe Fresco, Notability, or GoodNotes. Using the stylus, write each character in its box as you would write with a real pen on paper. When complete, export the file as a PNG at full resolution.

This method can produce excellent results if you use a high quality stylus and write in your natural style. It does not work well with a finger — only use a proper stylus.

---

### 2.4 Scan the Completed Template

Once every character box is filled in, you need to convert the paper template back into a digital file that can be uploaded to Calligraphr.

**Option 1 — Flatbed scanner (best quality)**

If you have access to a flatbed scanner, this is the best method. Place each page face-down on the scanner glass. Scan at a minimum of 300 DPI. Higher resolution such as 400 or 600 DPI produces even better results. Save the scanned file as a PNG or TIFF. Do not save as JPEG as this format introduces compression artefacts that can distort letter shapes.

**Option 2 — Phone document scanner app (recommended for most users)**

Download one of the following free apps on your phone:

Microsoft Lens is available on both iOS and Android. It automatically detects page edges, corrects perspective distortion, and enhances contrast. Open the app, select Document mode, point the camera at the template page on a flat surface, and tap the capture button. The app will process the image automatically. Export each scanned page as a PNG file.

Adobe Scan is also available on both iOS and Android and works similarly to Microsoft Lens. Open the app, tap the camera icon, and scan each page. Adobe Scan automatically detects document edges and enhances the image. Export as PNG.

**Option 3 — Phone camera (acceptable if scanner not available)**

Place the template page on a flat surface such as a table or floor. Ensure the lighting is bright and even with no shadows crossing the page. Hold your phone directly above the page so the camera is parallel to the paper. Do not tilt the phone at an angle. Take the photo at the highest resolution your phone supports.

After taking the photo, inspect it carefully on your phone screen. All boxes and characters must be clearly legible, the page must appear flat without distortion, and no shadows should cross any character boxes. Retake the photo if any of these conditions are not met.

If your phone has a built-in document scanner feature in the camera app, use that instead of a regular photo. This feature is often found in the camera app under the Scan Documents option on iPhone, or in the Samsung or Google camera apps on Android.

Regardless of which method you use, ensure that each page of the template is captured as a separate image file before proceeding.

---

### 2.5 Upload the Template to Calligraphr

Log in to your Calligraphr account and go to My Fonts in the navigation menu. Click Upload Template.

A file selector will open. Select the scanned image file for the first page of your template. If your template has multiple pages, you will upload them one at a time.

After uploading, Calligraphr will process the image and display a preview showing each detected character in its box. Review this preview carefully.

For each character, check that Calligraphr has correctly identified the letter in the box and that the extracted shape looks accurate. If a character looks distorted, is missing, or has been extracted with extra marks from the box border, click on that character in the preview to open the character editor. In the editor you can adjust the bounding box around the letter, rotate the character, or remove unwanted marks.

If a large number of characters look incorrect, it is better to go back and rescan the template at a higher resolution rather than trying to fix each character individually.

Repeat the upload process for each additional page of your template if you have multiple pages.

---

### 2.6 Build and Download Your Font

Once all your characters are uploaded and you are satisfied with the preview, you are ready to generate the font file.

From the My Fonts page in Calligraphr, click Build Font next to your character set. A settings dialog will appear.

Give your font a name. Use a simple name without spaces, such as MyHandwriting or YourFirstName. This name will be embedded in the font file but does not affect how it works in Assignmate.

In the font format options, ensure that TTF is selected or checked. Assignmate requires a TTF file. If OTF is also offered, you may select it as a second format, but TTF must be included.

Click Build Font and wait. The build process typically takes between 10 and 45 seconds depending on server load. Do not close the page while it is building.

When the build is complete, a download button will appear. Click Download Font. Save the TTF file to your computer. This is your personal handwriting font file.

Keep this file in a safe location. You will need to upload it to Assignmate in the next step, and you may want to use it again in the future if you create a new Assignmate account or need to re-upload it.

**Video resources for this step**

Search YouTube for the following to find video demonstrations of the Calligraphr build process:

### 2.7 Import Your Font into Assignmate

Log in to your Assignmate account and go to your dashboard. Click Handwriting Profile in the navigation menu or on the dashboard panel. This opens your profile settings page.

On the profile settings page, click Upload Font File. A file selector will open. Navigate to where you saved your TTF file from Calligraphr and select it.

Assignmate will upload the file and validate it. This takes a few seconds. If the file is valid, a font preview will appear on the screen showing a sample sentence rendered in your handwriting font. Read the preview carefully.

Compare the preview to your actual handwriting. The letters should look like yours. If the preview looks correct, click Save Profile. Your handwriting font is now active in Assignmate.

If the preview does not look like your handwriting, this is most commonly caused by one of the following issues:

The template was filled in too neatly or in a different style from your natural writing. Go back to Calligraphr, print a new template, fill it in more naturally, rescan, and rebuild the font.

The scan quality was too low, causing letter shapes to be incorrectly extracted. Rescan the template at a higher resolution using a flatbed scanner or a document scanner app and re-upload to Calligraphr.

Some characters were not filled in, causing them to appear as blank spaces. Check your Calligraphr character set and fill in any missing characters.

Once your profile is saved and the font looks correct, you are ready to generate your first handwritten assignment. Return to your dashboard and click New Assignment to begin.

## 3. Troubleshooting

**My handwriting photo was rejected during upload**

Ensure the file format is JPG, PNG, or WEBP and the file size is under 10 MB. If the file is too large, open it in a photo editor or use an online image compressor to reduce the file size before uploading.

**The AI analysis of my handwriting photo failed**

This can happen if the image is too blurry, too dark, or contains too much visual noise. Retake the photo in brighter lighting, hold the camera still, and ensure the handwriting is clearly legible in the image before uploading.

**Calligraphr is not detecting my characters correctly**

This is almost always caused by low scan quality. Rescan the template at 300 DPI or higher. If you used a phone camera, try a dedicated document scanner app such as Microsoft Lens or Adobe Scan instead. Also ensure the template was printed cleanly and that your pen marks are dark and clear.

**Some characters appear blank in the font preview**

This means those character boxes were not detected during the Calligraphr upload. Go back to My Fonts in Calligraphr and check which characters are missing. You can manually add individual characters by clicking the empty slot and uploading or drawing the character directly in the character editor.

**The font does not look like my handwriting**

The most common cause is that you wrote in a different style when filling in the template. Print a fresh template and fill it in while writing as naturally and casually as possible, the same way you would jot down a quick note. Also check that you used a consistent pen with a single stroke weight throughout the template.

**My downloaded PDF has blank spaces where letters should be**

This is caused by missing characters in your Calligraphr font. Open your Calligraphr account, check which characters are missing from your font, fill them in, rebuild the font, download the new TTF file, and re-upload it to Assignmate using the Handwriting Profile page.

**I cannot find my downloaded TTF file**

Check your Downloads folder first. If you remember saving it somewhere specific, search your computer for files ending in .ttf. If you cannot find the file, log back into Calligraphr and go to My Fonts. You can rebuild and download your font again from the same character set at any time without having to redo the template.

**My assignment generation is taking a long time**

Generation time depends on the length of the assignment and current server load. Assignments of 500 words or more may take up to 60 seconds. Do not close the browser tab while generation is running. If the page shows an error after more than 90 seconds, try generating again.

**I want to update my handwriting font after uploading it**

Go to Handwriting Profile on your dashboard and click Upload Font File again. Uploading a new TTF file will replace your existing font. All future assignments will use the new font. Previously generated assignments are not affected.
